Tulsi - The Queen of Herbs

Discover the benefits, uses, and growing conditions of this sacred herb revered in Ayurveda.

Overview

The Tulsi plant, also known as Holy Basil, is a sacred herb in Indian culture and Ayurvedic medicine. It is renowned for its powerful healing properties and has been used for centuries to treat various ailments.

Botanical Details

  • Scientific Name: Ocimum tenuiflorum
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Origin: Indian Subcontinent
  • Types: Rama Tulsi, Krishna Tulsi, Vana Tulsi

Key Benefits

  • Boosts immunity
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Supports respiratory health
  • Natural anti-inflammatory
  • Rich in antioxidants

Uses

  • Tea for relaxation and detox
  • Herbal remedies for colds and flu
  • Skin treatments for acne and wounds
  • Aromatherapy for stress relief
  • Traditional rituals in Indian culture

Growing Conditions

Tulsi thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. Regular watering and occasional pruning help the plant flourish. It can be grown in pots or gardens and is an excellent addition to any herbal collection.
