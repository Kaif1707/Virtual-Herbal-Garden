Calendula - The Skin-Soothing Herb

Discover the soothing, healing properties of Calendula, an herb known for its skin-care benefits and its ability to promote healing.

Overview

Calendula, also known as marigold, is widely used in skincare products for its anti-inflammatory and healing properties. It is known to soothe irritated skin, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

Botanical Details

  • Scientific Name: Calendula officinalis
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Origin: Europe, North Africa
  • Types: Common Marigold, Pot Marigold

Key Benefits

  • Soothes and heals irritated skin
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Helps treat cuts, burns, and rashes
  • Promotes wound healing
  • Has antioxidant and antimicrobial properties

Uses

  • Calendula oil for soothing skin irritations
  • Used in creams and lotions for healing purposes
  • Calendula tea for digestive health
  • Incorporated in ointments for cuts and burns
  • Used in aromatherapy for relaxation

Growing Conditions

Calendula prefers sunny areas with well-drained soil. It is a hardy plant that grows well in a variety of climates and can be easily grown in garden beds or containers. Regular watering and deadheading will encourage continuous blooming.
